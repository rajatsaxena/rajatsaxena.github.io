About

I am currently a 1st year PhD student in Neurobiology and Behavior department, UC Irvine. I joined Dr Bruce McNaughton's lab for my thesis and am working on understanding the interaction between the hippocampus and neocortex. Previously, I worked as Research Assistant at CNS in IISc, Bangalore where my projects were targeted towards understanding spatial information processing in the hippocampal formation. I was also involved in developing an animal behavior tracking system in large environments.


Intrigued by almost everything and anything related to engineering, mathematics, philosophy, design and psychology. I like to hack around building intelligent and interactive system using a combination of machine learning and computer vision. Most of my non-coding time is spent watching tv shows, reading, playing football or rock climbing.
