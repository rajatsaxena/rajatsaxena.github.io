About

I am currently a 2nd year PhD student in the Neurobiology and Behavior department at UC Irvine, advised by Dr Bruce L McNaughton. My research focuses on figuring out how short term memories are consolidated into long term memories through the hippocampal-cortical ensemble interaction. I use a combination of techniques including multi-contact probes, 2-photon imaging and machine learning models to understand continual learning and memory consolidation in the brain. Previously, I was a Research Assistant at IISc, Bangalore in Dr Sachin Deshmukh's lab studying the neural correlates of spatial navigation and memory. During that time, I also developed camera systems to track rodents in large spaces.


Intrigued by almost everything related to engineering, mathematics, philosophy, design and psychology. I like to hack around building machine vision and behavior tracking systems. Most of my non-coding time is spent reading, playing soccer, rock climbing or watching tv shows.
