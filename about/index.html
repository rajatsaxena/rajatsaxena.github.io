About

I am a third year PhD student in the Neurobiology and Behavior department at UC Irvine, advised by Dr. Bruce L. McNaughton and Dr. Sunil P. Gandhi . My research focuses on studying how short term memories are consolidated into long term memories through the hippocampal-cortical ensemble interaction. I use a combination of techniques including multi-contact probes, 2-photon imaging and machine learning models to understand continual learning and memory consolidation in the brain.

Previously, I was a Research Assistant at IISc, Bangalore in Dr. Sachin Deshmukh's lab studying the neural correlates of spatial navigation and memory. During that time, I developed camera systems to track rodents in large spaces . I received my bachelor's degree in Information Systems from Birla Institute of Technology & Science - Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, India in 2015.

When I am not working, I enjoy playing soccer, salsa dancing, rock climbing, and watching tv shows/anime. I love discussions on almost everything related to engineering, mathematics, philosophy, and design.
