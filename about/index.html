About

I am a fourth year PhD student in the Neurobiology and Behavior department at UC Irvine, advised by Dr. Bruce L. McNaughton . I'm currently a fourth-year Neuroscience graduate student in the McNaughton lab at the University of California, Irvine. My research focuses on studying how short-term memories are consolidated into long-term memories by looking at the hippocampus-neocortex ensemble interaction. I use a combination of techniques, including high-density silicon probes, 2-photon imaging, and deep learning models, to understand continual learning and memory consolidation in the brain. I also got to spend Summer 2020 working remotely on developing single-unit classifier for Kilosort2 spike sorting algoirthm with Dr. Marius Pachitariu.

Previously, I was a Research Assistant at IISc, Bangalore in Dr. Sachin Deshmukh's lab studying the neural correlates of spatial navigation and memory. During that time, I developed camera systems to track rodents in large spaces . I received my bachelor's degree in Information Systems from BITS Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, India in 2015.

When I am not working, I enjoy playing soccer, electric guitar, salsa dancing, rock climbing, and watching tv shows/anime. I love discussions on almost everything related to engineering, mathematics, philosophy, and design.
